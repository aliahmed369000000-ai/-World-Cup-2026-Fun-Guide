2. US VISA — Don't Forget This

Egyptian passport holders require a B1/B2 US Tourist Visa. Here are the current costs and requirements:

ItemCost (USD)Notes
US Visa Application Fee (MRV)$185Non-refundable, paid online
SEVIS Fee (if applicable)$0Not required for tourist visa
Travel to US Embassy$10–50Transport to Cairo embassy
Passport photos$5–102 photos required
Total Visa Cost~$200Apply 3+ months in advance

Apply for your US visa at least 3 months in advance. Wait times at the US Embassy in Cairo have increased significantly due to high World Cup demand. Book your visa appointment as soon as possible.

Canadian visa costs approximately $100 CAD and Mexican tourist entry is free for Egyptian passport holders (no visa required for up to 180 days).

MONEY SAVING TIPS

1. Get Free Tickets First: Check our free ticket programs guide — Verizon and DoorDash are giving away thousands of free tickets. This could save you $200-500.

2. Book Flights Now: Prices are rising every week. Book as early as possible and set up price alerts on Aviasales or Google Flights.

3. Stay in Airbnb: For group trips (3-4 people), an Airbnb near the stadium is often cheaper per person than a hotel and gives you a kitchen to save on food costs.

4. Mexico City is Cheapest: If you have flexibility on which matches to attend, Mexico City hotels and food are significantly cheaper than US cities. The Estadio Azteca Group Stage matches are the most affordable World Cup experience.

5. Use Public Transport: Never take a taxi on match day. Use metro/subway. It's faster, cheaper and part of the experience.

6. Travel eSIM: Don't use roaming — it can cost $15-20/day. A travel eSIM from Airalo costs $5/day and works in all 3 host countries.
